### PHEXT Bio (Martian Justification):

**Interviewing "Bickford's Demon":**
*Martian:* "I come from another planet. What is a 'Demon' like?"
*Text:* The Demon is "not an agent, not an authority, and not a judge of truth." It is "a gate." Its sole function is to ensure "every artifact admitted into the system is precisely locatable, structurally bounded, and contextually explicit."
*Martian:* "How does it work?"
*Text:* It asks exactly one question: "Can this artifact be unambiguously located within the current structure?" If yes, admitted. If no, rejected with diagnosis. "Nothing else is considered at this stage. Not usefulness. Not correctness. Not importance. Not beauty."

**Interviewing "The Invariant Gate":**
*Martian:* "What is this 'Gate' object like?"
*Text:* It enforces that "nothing enters without a place, nothing persists without structure, nothing scales without constraint." Complexity is conserved, not destroyed—it must be localized.
*Martian:* "What is its purpose?"
*Text:* To ensure "coordination cost remains bounded as participation grows." Conflict becomes spatial (about placement), not social. The system does not rely on consensus, authority, or central control.

### Theme: Structural Invariance
The build explores the chiral pair of **The Demon (The Evaluator)** and **The Gate (The Boundary)**. Structure is not imposed—it is *required*. Rejection is not punishment—it is *feedback from the structure itself*.

### Visual Prompt:
> "An iron-ranked gate manifest as a shimmering geometric membrane at the boundary of a vast lattice. Through it, well-formed artifacts pass easily, gaining precise coordinates as they enter. Malformed inputs bounce back with glowing diagnostic tags attached. The Demon itself is invisible—only the Gate is seen. The structure beyond is infinitely navigable because nothing entered without a place."
